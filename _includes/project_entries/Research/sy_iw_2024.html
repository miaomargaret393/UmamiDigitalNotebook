research

My Sophomore Year Independent Work, I contributed to the now published research Engineering Cellular Self-Adhesions Inside 3D Printed Micro-Arches to Enhance Cell:Biomaterial Attachment, where we designed 3D micro-tunnels that induced cellular deformation over themselves using their own E-cadherin (“handshake”) proteins.

These “Self-Adhesion Tunnels” (SATs) were arch-shaped structures that let cells wrap underneath and strengthen their attachment, inhibiting migration. I proposed the trapezoidal design, which achieved the highest adhesion efficiency (about 93%) by using a wider base that tapers inwards to a sharp edge at the top to promote cellular gripping and wrapping. Furthermore, I contributed to refining the fabrication process of these geometries on the Nanoscribe, optimizing laser parameters for clean, fast prints.

The work showed that purely structural cues, without added chemical coatings, can significantly enhance cell–material attachment, offering new strategies for improving tissue interfaces and implant integration.
