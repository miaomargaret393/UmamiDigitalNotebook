My research interests center on designing biomaterials and microarchitectures that guide cell behavior, support regeneration, and communicate with dynamic biological environments. I am interested in combining nano-engineering with biomaterials to create platforms for drug testing, organ-on-chip models, and regenerative interfaces that reduce reliance on animal testing. More broadly, I aim to link material design with clinical translation by developing systems that function reliably in real therapeutic and diagnostic settings.

I primarily work with digital art nowadays, but I also enjoy traditional media (watercolor, oil painting, etc.) and sketching.
