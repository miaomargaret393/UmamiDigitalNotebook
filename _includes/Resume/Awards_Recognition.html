Awards and Recognition

Awards

    MAE Department, Princeton University

    My undergraduate thesis, "Topographic Control of Cell Migration: A Nanofabricated 3D Imaging Platform for Biomaterial and Device Interface Engineering," was awarded the Morgan W. McKinzie '93 Senior Thesis Prize for the best senior thesis in the MAE department.

  • Building Bridges Award

Fellowships

  • Sigma Xi

    May 2026

    Nominated into Sigma Xi, The Scientific Research Honor Society, which is one of the oldest and largest multidisciplinary honor societies for scientists and engineers, with a membership history that includes over 200 Nobel laureates.

  • NSF Graduate Research Fellowship

    April 2026

    Awarded the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship (GRFP), the oldest graduate fellowship of its kind in the U.S. and widely considered the gold standard among STEM graduate fellowships.

  • Schwarzman Scholars

    November 2025

    Selected for the 11th Cohort of Schwarzman Scholars, a fully-funded master's program at Tsinghua University in Beijing designed to prepare the next generation of global leaders. The Class of 2026–27 was selected from a record-high pool of nearly 5,800 applicants worldwide — the most competitive cycle in the program's history — narrowed to 150 scholars, an acceptance rate of roughly 2.6%. I was awarded a place, but declined due to a scheduling conflict.

  • TIME Initiative

    2025 TIME Fellow (November 2024–Present)

    I was selected as a TIME 2025 Fellow, a program sponsored by the Academy for Health and Lifespan Research that supports individuals advancing aging biology. As one of only two engineers in the cohort, I brought a biomaterials perspective to a field dominated by biology and medicine, presenting on how engineered microenvironments can reduce cellular senescence and support healthier tissue function, and introducing microprinting methods to precisely control the cues that influence cellular aging. The fellowship builds a network of emerging leaders working to reduce age-related disease.
